About

I move between the framework and the floor.

Cambridge education, ACCA qualified, ten years inside scaling companies on three continents — and a stand-up habit that taught me more about audiences than any deck ever did.

The arcHow I got here

I ran operations at Careem, Bolt, Motive and Wise — the kind of roles where you learn how scaled companies actually work: the pricing logic, the expansion playbooks, the incident response, the cost discipline. The frameworks that make a 10-person decision survive a 1,000-person company.

Then I noticed the gap. The founders and small teams I respected most were drowning in the exact problems those frameworks solve — but had no access to them. They were told to "hustle" when what they needed was a system.

So I started Soch with my co-founders to close that gap, using AI automation as the delivery mechanism. The thesis hasn't changed since day one: AI amplifies the quality of thinking you already have. It's leverage, not a shortcut.

The other halfOff the org chart

Estonia, comedy, and proof-of-concepts.

I've been based in Tallinn since late 2021, working toward Estonian residency and grinding through B1 Estonian one Anki deck at a time.

I do stand-up, and I make bilingual content that code-switches between Urdu and English — the comedy background is why I care more about whether a thing lands than whether it looks clever.

When I wanted to prove that anyone can build with AI now, I shipped a browser-based, hand-tracking arcade game with zero prior coding experience. The point wasn't the game. The point was: the barrier moved, and most people haven't noticed yet.
