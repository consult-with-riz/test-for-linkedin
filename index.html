01 — PremiseWhy any of this exists

Soch means "to think" in Urdu.

The companies I worked for had frameworks for everything — pricing, expansion, incident response, the lot. Small teams don't get access to that machinery. They get advice.

So the work now is plumbing: AI workflow automation that does the repetitive thinking, so the founder gets their hours back for the thinking only they can do. Built on n8n and the Anthropic API, shipped, owned by you.

02 — BeliefsThe contrarian bits

What I actually think.

  1. AI amplifies thinking. It doesn't replace it.Point a sloppy process at a model and you get faster slop. The quality of the input thinking sets the ceiling.
  2. Automation is an operations problem, not a tooling problem.The tool is the last 10%. The map of what should happen, in what order, with what guardrails — that's the work.
  3. Frameworks beat heroics.Scaled companies win on repeatable systems, not on one brilliant person staying late. Founders deserve the same.
  4. Ship the proof, not the pitch.I built an arcade game with no coding background to prove a point. Working things persuade. Decks don't.
